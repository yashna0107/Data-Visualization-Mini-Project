Bar Charts

Bar 1: Comparison of the number of male student enrollments in various field of study in 1992 and 2009

The above bar chart compares the number of male students enrolled in different field of study in the year 1992 and 2009. It is seen that the Humanities, Social and behavioural sciences and Law, business, and architecture, engineering and related technologies have been a field of interest for males in both 1992 and 2009. However, the number of enrollement have increased significantly between those years. Admisisons in Business and Architecture and related technologies have seen a substantial rise by 32% and 31% in 2009 respectively. On the other hand, there has been a marginal increase of 9% admission for mathematics and computer science by male students.

Bar 2: Comparison of the number of female student enrollments in diverse of study in 1992 and 2009

Bar Chart 2 demonstrates the comparison of the number of female students enrolled in different programs in the year 1992 and 2009. Humanities, Business, Social and behavioural science and Law, and Education has been a field of interest among the female students. However,the interest to study in these field remained the same in the year 2009 with substantial rise in admissions of 18%, 33% and 32% in Humanities, Business and, Social and behavioural and Law respectively. Admisisons in Education program saw a marginal decrease of 11% in 2009. On the contrary, there was a sudden rise of enrollments in Health and recreation program by 62% in 2009 compared to 1992.

Bar 3: Total number of enrollments by registration status in the year 1992 and 2009

The bar chart displays the comparison between part-time and full-time student enrollements in 1992 and 2009. It is visible that the number of full time status student rose by 37% in 2009. The number of full time students in both 1992 and 2009 were higher compared to the part-time. However, the number of part-time student in 1992 and 2009 show slight difference of 10%. In fact, the number of part-time student enrollment decreased in 2009.

Bar 4: Total number of enrollments by Program level in the year 1992 and 2009

The bar chart compares enrollements in different program levels in 1992 and 2009. It is visible that the number of students in Undergraduate and Bachelors level were higher than the rest. However, the percentage increased by 19% and 24% in 2009 for both the program levels respectively. Number of Graduate level student admitted were only 109,494 in 1992 which increased by 43% to 170076 in 2009.
